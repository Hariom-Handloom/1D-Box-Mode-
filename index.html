1D-Box Model

1D Box Model (Particle in a Box)

The 1D box model in quantum mechanics describes a particle (such as an electron) confined within a rigid, one-dimensional box of length (𝐿). The box has impenetrable walls, meaning the particle cannot exist outside it. This model provides insights into energy quantization and wavefunctions in confined systems.

Assumptions of the Model

  1. The particle is free to move within the box along one dimension.
  2. The potential energy inside the box is V(x)=0, and it is infinite (V(x)=∞) outside the box.
  3. The particle cannot escape the box, leading to boundary conditions at x=0 and x=L.
  4. The particle exhibits wave-like behavior governed by the Schrödinger equation.

Schrödinger Equation

The time-independent Schrödinger equation for a particle is:

-ħ2/2m (d2ψ(x)/dx2) + V(x)ψ(x) = Eψ(x)

Inside the 1D box where \( V(x) = 0 \), the equation simplifies to:

d2ψ(x)/dx2 + k2ψ(x) = 0

General Solution

Inside a one-dimensional box where the potential energy \( V(x) = 0 \), the Schrödinger equation simplifies to:

d2ψ(x)/dx2 + k2ψ(x) = 0

The general solution of this second-order differential equation is:

ψ(x) = A sin(kx) + B cos(kx)

Here, \( A \) and \( B \) are constants to be determined using boundary conditions, and \( k \) is related to the energy \( E \) by:

k = √(2mE)/ħ

Boundary Conditions

For a particle in a 1D box of length \( L \), the boundary conditions are:

ψ(0) = 0 and ψ(L) = 0

Applying these conditions to the general solution:

ψ(0) = A sin(0) + B cos(0) = B = 0

This means the solution simplifies to:

ψ(x) = A sin(kx)

Applying the second boundary condition \( ψ(L) = 0 \):

ψ(L) = A sin(kL) = 0

For \( A \neq 0 \), we must have \( sin(kL) = 0 \), which gives:

kL = nπ, where n = 1, 2, 3, ...

Therefore:

k = nπ/L

Quantized Energy Levels

Using the relation \( k = √(2mE)/ħ \) and substituting \( k = nπ/L \), the quantized energy levels of the particle are:

En = (n²π²ħ²) / (2mL²), where n = 1, 2, 3, ...

The quantum number \( n \) represents the energy level of the particle, and the lowest energy level corresponds to \( n = 1 \).

Normalized Wavefunction

The normalized wavefunction \( ψn(x) \) for the particle is:

ψn(x) = √(2/L) sin(nπx/L), where n = 1, 2, 3, ...

The normalization ensures that the total probability of finding the particle inside the box is 1.
